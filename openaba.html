In the current state of healthcare industries, behavioral therapists and practitioners have to continue working to provide critical essential services to our community for the population and individuals with autism and other special needs who are more susceptible to getting the virus.


During times of shelter-in-place, the need for quality and accessible tele-heath for behavioral therapists and their clients is greater than ever.


This is why healthcare services like Applied Behavior Analysis are so important.

Efficacy of ABA session
explained

ABA sessions typically consist of a variety of activities designed to develop verbal and reasoning skills, and are guided via a trained therapist.



Trained providers are usually either a registered behavior technician (RBT), or a board-certified behavior analyst (BCBA). The provider(s) plans activities (also called "targets"), evaluates client responses (the “behavior” in Applied Behavior Analysis), and delivers intermittent rewards (called "reinforcers").

Constraints

Controls on tooling in this increasingly specialized field vary by payer and state: though some states and insurance companies have relaxed requirements on diligence on HIPAA compliance, many states still have strict HIPAA-alignment on tools enabling services via tele-health, requiring a business associate agreement (BAA) between providers seeking reimbursement and said provider's software vendors.

Fueled by Token-Based
Learning

OpenABA provides an intuitive session constructor that replicates the strategies of token-based learning. These sessions can generate interactive decks that does everything that a physical card or token can with the added benefit of limitless customization, digital organization, and convenient cloning capabilities.

Targets

Goals are grouped into targets. To reorder targets, they can be dragged and dropped into the desired categories and navigation between the targets in any order is possible during the session.
